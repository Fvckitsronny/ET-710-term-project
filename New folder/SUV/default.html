A sport utility vehicle or suburban utility vehicle (SUV) is a vehicle similar to a station wagon or estate car, usually equipped with four-wheel drive for on- or off-road ability. Some SUVs include the towing capacity of a pickup truck with the passenger-carrying space of a minivan or large sedan. The Jeep Cherokee of 1983 and especially the Ford Explorer of 1990 made SUVs popular for mothers and families in the late 1980s and early 1990s, despite their reputation as dangerous vehicles  (though minivans have done poorly on safety tests as well). By 1994 SUVs outsold minivans in the United States. Peaking in the late 1990s and early to mid 2000s, SUVs sales temporarily declined due to high oil prices and a declining economy. The traditional truck-based SUV is gradually being supplanted by the crossover SUV, which uses an automobile platform for lighter weight and better fuel efficiency, as a response to much of the criticism of sport utility vehicles.
